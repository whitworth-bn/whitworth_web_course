Katrina Costales

Major: Human-Computer Interaction

What brought me to Whitworth?

The day I knew I was going to Whitworth was when my soccer coach created a personalized campus tour of his favorite spots. I felt a sense of community and belonging and knew that God was guiding me to take a leap of faith.

I was admitted into Whitworth with design/architectural interests, and I grew passionate about the Technology and Design realm.

"Where do you want me to be, Lord? How can I serve? How can I use my gifts to help people?" I prayed and reflected, growing a passionate heart to serve and make the most of the opportunities blessed to me.

Why did I choose my major?

Growing up, I dreamed of becoming an architect, inspired by my father, a welder who brought his sketches to life. However, the COVID-19 pandemic disrupted pivotal high school experiences, and I realized I didn't enjoy my online AP Statistics and AP Calculus classes.

I found my passion in human-computer interaction, where I balance user-centric design with software problem-solving and ethnographic research. It's a field that allows me to merge creativity with technology.

"Technology should support human flourishing, not dictate it."

Future Plans

After graduation, I plan to work as a UX/UI designer, reimagining how humans interact with technology. I hope to contribute to digital experiences that are intuitive, accessible, and impactful.
